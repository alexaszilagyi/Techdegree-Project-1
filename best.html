My Best Coding Experience

This experience isn't a professional one, but a personal revelation.

In April 2014, I found Treehouse. I signed up for the basic account, and as time allowed, I took the Web Design course. As I mentioned in my Worst Coding Experience, I had taken two responsive web design courses in college, but there were some concepts I just wasn't grasping.

It didn't take long for me to realize joining Treehouse was an invaluable step for me. The concepts I didn't understand in college, I was understanding now. I noticed I was much faster at coding landing pages for internal use at work, and my confidence with writing code was increasing.

I started to wonder "why." Why was I able to grasp concepts in a few months with Treehouse that I didn't understand in a year at college? After some thought, I figured it out:

  • With Treehouse, I was learning on my time. In college, I had 5 classes per semester, which meant my focus was being pulled in at least 5 different directions. Yes, I was working full-time (as I still am), but I got to leave work at work which freed up time and energy to focus on learning code.
  • I was learning at my pace. When I couldn't figure something out, I could pause the Treehouse video I was watching to search for and read supplemental information. Please note, I don't hold this against my web professor in college; he made himself as available to us students as possible, but the bottom line was that he had a curriculum to follow.
  • Being in control of my education made me feel empowered. Yes, I chose what college to attend, but as I mentioned above, colleges mean curriculums. Schools have to cover a certain amount of information, regardless of the information's relativity to a student's major. With Treehouse, I am completely in control of my education, which makes me feel far more successful and confident in my endeavors.
